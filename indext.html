Savitribai Phule

Pioneer of Women's Education in India

About Savitribai

Savitribai Phule was a great Indian social reformer, poet and teacher. She is widely known as the first female teacher of India.

In 1848, along with her husband Jyotirao Phule, she opened the first school for girls in Pune. At a time when women's education was not accepted, she fought against discrimination and worked for equality.

Her contribution to education and social reform continues to inspire millions across India.

"Sit idle no more. Go, get education."

Major Achievements

1848

Opened the first school for girls in Pune.

1852

Started multiple schools for girls and marginalized communities.

1863

Established a care center for pregnant widows.

1897

Helped plague patients and sacrificed her life in service.
